# Math Genius — Villain Profiles

Profiles of recurring villains who don't live in the town but occasionally show up to cause mischief or attempt a takeover.

---

## Jafar
**Origin:** Aladdin  
**Status:** Powerless (no magic)  
**Personality:** Scheming and pompous, Jafar still carries himself with the haughty air of a sorcerer even though he no longer has any magic. He is determined to take over the town through cunning and manipulation alone, though without his powers he tends to be far less effective than he imagines. He is persistent and never seems to learn from his repeated failures.

---

## Bowser
**Origin:** Super Mario  
**Preferred Mode of Transport:** Airship  
**Personality:** Loud, boisterous, and genuinely dangerous. Bowser arrives in style on his airship and makes no secret of his intentions — he wants to conquer the town. His brute strength and heavily armed airship make him a serious threat, even if his plans rarely go smoothly once the neighborhood kids get involved.

---

## Discord
**Origin:** My Little Pony  
**Species:** Unicorn  
**Role:** Chaos coordinator  
**Personality:** Discord is a mischief-maker who delights in disorder. Rather than taking over the town himself, he prefers to recruit and coordinate other villains to do his bidding, weaving elaborate schemes designed to bewilder and unsettle the townsfolk. He is particularly annoyed by Fluffy and Humphrey — their easy-going, unflappable natures mean his chaos barely registers with them, which frustrates him to no end. When his schemes fail to faze those two, it tends to unravel his whole plan.

---

## Lego Humphrey
**Origin:** Kiki's laboratory (escaped experiment)  
**Size:** Same size as Humphrey  
**Speed:** Just as fast as Humphrey  
**Personality:** Lego Humphrey is a living construction of Lego bricks — a rogue experiment that escaped from Kiki's lab. It cannot speak, but it doesn't need to. It is mischievous by nature and delights in causing mayhem wherever it goes. What makes it especially tricky to stop is its ability to reassemble itself: if it is knocked apart, its pieces find their way back together on their own. It can also reshape itself mid-encounter, reconfiguring its Lego pieces into entirely new forms — vehicles, creatures, structures — much like a living nanomachine made of bricks. Its relentless speed and shape-shifting ability make it one of the more unpredictable threats the neighborhood faces.
